How keyword density should be used

Keyword density shows how often a focus phrase appears compared with the total word count. It can help you notice missing topics or repeated wording, but it should not be treated as a strict ranking formula.

Good SEO writing answers the search intent naturally. If a keyword appears too often, the content may feel forced. If it never appears, search engines and readers may not understand the main topic.

What to check

  • Whether the main phrase appears naturally in the page.
  • Whether repeated wording makes the content awkward.
  • Whether related terms and examples support the topic.
  • Whether headings match what the page actually explains.

What keyword density is best?

There is no universal best percentage. Natural, helpful content is more important than hitting a fixed density number.

Can high keyword density hurt a page?

Overusing the same phrase can make content hard to read and may look manipulative. Keep wording natural.
