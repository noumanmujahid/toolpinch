Why a sitemap helps

An XML sitemap lists important URLs on a website. It helps search engines discover pages, especially on new sites or sites with many internal pages. A sitemap does not guarantee indexing, but it gives crawlers a clear list to review.

Use only canonical URLs in your sitemap. If your preferred domain is https://www.example.com, keep every sitemap URL on that same domain and format.

Good sitemap habits

  • Include important public pages only.
  • Use canonical clean URLs.
  • Keep blocked or duplicate pages out of the sitemap.
  • Submit the sitemap in Search Console after deployment.

When to update it

Update your sitemap when you add, remove or rename important pages. After a major update, resubmit the same sitemap URL in Search Console.

Does a sitemap guarantee indexing?

No. It helps discovery, but Google still evaluates page quality and crawl signals.

Include important canonical pages. Leave out duplicate, private or low-value URLs.
