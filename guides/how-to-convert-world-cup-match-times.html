How to Convert World Cup Match Times

International football schedules can be confusing because host cities use different timezones. For the 2026 tournament, matches are played across Canada, Mexico and the United States, so one fixed conversion does not work for every venue.

The safest method is to select the host city timezone, enter the published local kickoff date and time, then convert it to your own timezone. A timezone-aware converter also handles daylight-saving rules for the selected date.

Common mistakes

  • Assuming every United States venue uses the same timezone.
  • Converting from a television listing that is already localized.
  • Ignoring a date change when the match begins after midnight locally.
  • Using a fixed UTC offset instead of a named timezone.

Before the match

Confirm the kickoff with the official competition schedule, then save the converted local date and time. Broadcasters may list pre-match coverage earlier than the actual kickoff.
