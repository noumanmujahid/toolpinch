How to Do Keyword Research for a New Website

Keyword research for a new website should start with useful problems, not only broad keywords. A new site usually has a better chance when it targets clear long-tail searches and creates pages that fully answer one intent.

Start with a seed topic, generate keyword ideas, group them by intent and choose the pages you can make genuinely helpful. Avoid creating many thin pages for very similar phrases. One strong page can often cover several close variations.

Simple workflow

  1. Write down 5 to 10 seed topics.
  2. Generate long-tail and question ideas for each topic.
  3. Group similar phrases by intent.
  4. Pick page ideas that match tools, guides or examples you can provide.
  5. Write titles, descriptions and internal links before publishing.

What to avoid

  • Do not trust fake search-volume numbers.
  • Do not publish many near-duplicate pages.
  • Do not choose keywords you cannot answer properly.
  • Do not ignore Search Console data after pages start getting impressions.
